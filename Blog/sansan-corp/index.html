Reminiscing the Sansan Cycle [CORP]
2015.12.25. - Data and Destiny

This is my second article on the SanSan cycle. I'm reviewing the most used corporation cards, focusing on current day competitive play. I sense a lot of pain and headache coming at the runners, I hope they are prepared. Fire up the ICE and rez those assets, we are going in!
You can find the first article about the runner cards here:
Reminiscing the Sansan Cycle [RUNNER]

The Valley

Those pesky facecheckers will pay! And by pesky facechecker I mean Anarchs. It's usually them free-spirited aggressors who like to go headfirst onto an unrezzed ICE without any breakers. It has strength 4, which is nice because Mimic is not enough to neutralize the subroutine. Unfortunately this card is pretty useless in late game, also Faust can mitigate its threat.

Breaker Bay

This is your anti-Anarch ICE since these days they are all about Faust. Turing will protect your scoring remote like no other. When positioned on centrals, it can also stop an Eater trying to squeeze in an Account Siphon or a Keyhole run. Since Anarchs are the current dominant runner faction, I would suggest putting 3 in any HB deck. Don't forget that the runner can click through. Considering the current meta, this is the best ICE of the cycle.

Running on the archives has never been so painful. Jinteki: Replicating Perfection was loving this. Use it to reinstall an economy asset (Sundew, Mental Health Clinic), a taxing upgrade (Ash 2X3ZB9CY, Caprice Nisei), ICE or even an agenda. It's cheap but does not end the run.

Insanely low-price tagging ICE. The runner will probably take the tag, there is no point in trying to break a strength 6 sentry or beat a trace 7. This is quite taxing if your opponent cares about tags. If not, one of you will be in trouble soon.

Combine it with the HB bots (Adonis Campaign, Eve Campaign) and you have saved a lot of credits. You can even put it into your scoring server next to Ash 2X3ZB9CY or Caprice Nisei. Remember, only 1 region per server, does not work with i.e. SanSan City Grid.

Chrome City

Probably the best face-up Weyland agenda there is. It's like clicking for money but in the meantime you are also advancing an agenda. You have to protect it well, since it is obvious to the runner that this card worth points. Big ICE and Ash 2X3ZB9CY should come in handy.

The Underway

They Weyland Consortium was always good with barriers. It taxes well, costs 3 credits to break with a Corroder or 2 counters with Cerberus "Lady" H1. Sadly, it is vunerable to Parasite.

It creates an unbreakable subroutine which you can only escape by a successful psi game. Damaging and program trashing subroutines are the best choice, but an "End the run" will also suffice in some cases. NEXT Gold can be a powerful candidate. Add some recursion (Crick, Interns) for extra fun.

Old Hollywood

This is an alternative to NAPD Contract. Though NAPD Contract's ability can actually stop the runner from stealing the agenda, Explode-a-Palooza provides almost the same economic advantage. Plus it might enable the kill for a butcher deck when both sides are broke. Haarpsichord Studios: Entertainment Unleashed can benefit from it even more if multiple agenda's "when accessed" ability fire.

The Universe of Tomorrow

You can put this in any horizontal deck, it is just 1 influence. Haas-Bioroid: Engineering the Future and Near-Earth Hub: Broadcast Center loves this by default. You can also fit it in into a Jinteki deck using 1-pointer agendas. Use Turtlebacks for extra profit. Reinstalling expensive-to-trash cards like SanSan City Grid or Daily Business Show really wrecks the runner economy. Ignoring install cost means you can save some credits if you are using multiple small ICE on centrals. This a real pain fo the runner, nice effect, cheap to rez, costing 4 to trash. It is the best asset of the cycle that you can fit into various decks.

The competition among 3/1 agendas are really tight. The ability to search for anything and not showing it to the runner is strong and flexible. This can nurture kill decks, fast advance decks and also some wicked combo shenanigans (Power Shutdown + Accelerated Diagnostics, I'm looking at you). How about pairing it up with Team Sponsorship to install the card you just searched for?

BFFs with Spark Agency: Worldswide Reach. Install it on a server and the runner will have to trash it, delivering a 4(5) credit advantage. It is also awesome as a random trap triggering on a central access. The only downside is deckspace.

Identities

I am happy that Haarpsichord Studios: Entertainment Unleashed from the cycle is truly a tier 1 identity enabling a lot of competitive decks. "Convenience Shop" is a successful archetype combining the annoying "when accessed" NBN agendas with 24/7 News Cycle and Weyland meat damage. The identity lets you care less about ICE and open up some interesting end game possibilities.

Jinteki Biotech: Life Imagined seems to be very flexible. You can play it as a rush deck or going for flatline, even a mixture of both. Unfortunately, Jinteki has seen some better days and the current metagame does not favour it. Cybernetics Division: Humanity Upgraded strives to bring the brain damage side of HB to life without much result. It can provide some surprises, but usually fails to deliver at tournaments.

Balance of factions (top 30% decks on tournaments)

Oh NBN. We thought you could be restrained by Clot. Players soon found out that with Cyberdex Virus Suite and Shipment from SanSan it can be mitigated. Butchershop decks also flourished, strengthening the NBN faction even more. The only competitor of NBN was HB thanks to Turing which successfully opposed the popular Anarch decks. Team Sponsorship (also awesome in NBN) and Breaker Bay Grid helped HB a little more. In the meantime the success of Jinteki: Replicating Perfection diminished and Weyland struggled hard on the borderline of failure. It is easy to see that NBN needs some restraining while Jinteki and Weyland desperately require some new good cards to regain competitiveness.
