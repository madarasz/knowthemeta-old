Reminiscing the Sansan Cycle [RUNNER]
2015.12.19. - Data and Destiny

Ah, the SanSan cycle! Each Data Pack is inspired by a specific part of the famous American megacity in the Android universe. What did the SanSan cycle bring us in addition to the flavor? Well, cards of course! In this article, I will be exploring the most used and most useful cards of the cycle focusing on current day competitive play.
You can find the second article about the corp cards here:
Reminiscing the Sansan Cycle [CORP]

The Valley

NBN fast advance is a force to be reckoned with these days. This archetype brought so much frustration and sparked many helpless cries from the runners. Something had to be done to stop the AstroScript Pilot Program trains in their tracks. Which brings us to the silver bullet named Clot. This card is mostly found in Shaper and Anarch decks utilizing instantaneous install ability of Clone Chip, Self-modifying Code and Street Peddler. It’s pretty much an auto-include in most of these decks. Unfortunately corps pack their own silver bullet against it, Cyberdex Virus Suite. These things balance themselves out nicely, but without Clot the fight against fast advance would be lost.

The mini Professional Contacts, luckily a Neutral resource without any faction cost. For 2 credits, it brings so much value. It’s mostly the tool of Shapers and Criminals while Anarchs tend to use Wyldside, Off-Campus Apartment and Drug Dealer for drawing.

The Adjusted Chronotype and Wyldside combo is the draw engine of numerous Anarch decks. Players even started calling an archetype “Pancake Noise” for utilizing it. It might take a time and resources to get the combination on the table, but the joy of two free cards every turn puts a smile on many faces. Don’t forget to include Faust in the picture for more giggles and fun.

Breaker Bay:

It’s like Modded for resources with a low faction cost. Anarchs and some Criminals run it, lowering the price of Daily Casts, Liberated Account, Earthrise Hotel and the just mentioned dream team of Adjusted Chronotype and Wyldside.

Chrome City:

Though it’s a Shaper hardware, it mostly complements the fixed-strength Anarch breakers Mimic and Yog.0. Lotus Field used to be a way to give Anarchs and Criminals a hard time. Net-Ready Eyes is a solution for that, but nowadays Faust is the king of Anarch breakers. Though it is still found in “L4J Whizzard“ decks.

The Underway:

Wow. This card gave Anarchs such an incredible boost, they still dominate the current metagame. It’s breaker which can be used even when you are broke on credits. It’s an AI breaker which can handle all ICE subtypes. Breaking costs cards from hand, but this program singlehandedly eliminates most safe places on the table. Turing is a nice counter against decks using Faust. This fact helped HB decks to establish their foothold in tournaments further on.

Another Anarch powerhouse. It is so good that vinegarymink even wrote An Entire Article About Street Peddler. Instantaneous install of programs, resources and hardware which keeps the corp guessing in fear. Does she have Clot, Clone Chip, Paparazzi or some breaker? They will find out eventually.

Old Hollywood:

It is cheap in credits, cheap in faction cost and packs a punch against NAPD Contract and The Future Perfect. Film Critic also protects you against Midseason Replacements and Punitive Counterstrike. It can strangle Haarpsichord Studios: Entertainment Unleashed and its “when the runner accesses…” agendas. Runners using Kate usually pack one, but it is sporadically found in other decks as well.

Finally, the most used Criminal card of the cycle. The cruel twist of fate is that Drug Dealer is usually found in the “DLR Valencia” archetype, made famous by the 2015 Worlds Champion Dan D’argenio. It plays together well with the Off-Campus Apartment, fitting in with the other connections. Drug Dealer gives a nice boost early game and really shines with Faust. It also offers some damage protection, ideally raising the cards in hand to 6 during the corporation’s turn.

In the midst of killer corps, this is the savior of tag-me runners. Paparazzi offers synergy with Data Leak Reversal (DLR), which is nice, since DLR is the tag-me archetype these days. It still needs Wireless Net Pavilion to be useful.

The Universe of Tomorrow:

This card enables DLR decks to be top-tier. Wireless Net Pavilion and Fall Guy make trashing resources so expensive in clicks and credits that most corporations will not bother. Especially if they are kept poor by Account Siphon.

Identities

Unfortunately the SanSan cycle did not offer any top-tier runner identities. Some players experiment with Hayley Kaplan: Universal Scholar achieving mixed results. The two Criminal identities, Armand "Geist" Walker: Tech Lord and Laramy Fisk: Savvy Investor are still not successful in competitive play. Hopefully in the future they will receive some cards that fit them well.

Balance of factions (top 30% decks on tournaments)

The indisputable winners of the SanSan cycle are Anarchs. Shapers are having their ups and downs but they show a steady participation in the top decks of tournaments. The Criminals are on a decline which makes me sad. Initially Anarchs are weakened by Turing. Later you can clearly see the tremendous impact of Faust and Street Peddler in the chart. The hype settles a bit as Shaper gets Film Critic., still Anarch has a good lead on the other factions. It’s worth noting that Kate "Mac" McCaffrey: Digital Tinker remains the most successful runner.
